## Kuroit Reviews

The thing about reading hosting reviews is that you've got to take them with the right mindset. Happy customers are usually too busy running their websites to leave reviews. Upset customers? They've got time to write essays.

That said, Kuroit's sitting at 4.1 stars on Trustpilot with 80+ reviews, which puts them in the "generally reliable" category. Here's what people are actually saying:

**The Good Stuff:**

Several reviewers mention the support team goes beyond basic troubleshooting. One customer noted a backup configuration issue was resolved within 20 minutes of opening a ticket. That's pretty responsive for a hosting company.

The value proposition gets mentioned a lot. People appreciate getting decent specs without paying premium prices. One review specifically called out that Kuroit's VPS quality matches or exceeds bigger names but costs less.

Migration services seem solid. Multiple customers mentioned free migrations that were handled smoothly and quickly, often within 24 hours.

**The Complaints:**

Network performance pops up as a complaint from some users, particularly mentioning high latency and occasional downtime lasting 2-3 days with slow support response during outages. A few customers felt abandoned when things went wrong.

Some reviews mention aggressive payment collection policies, with services getting suspended quickly after missed payments and additional late fees being charged.

The lower-tier VPS plans apparently run into storage limitations, with users noting that additional storage upgrades are expensive.

**What Industry Folks Say:**

On forums like LowEndTalk, Kuroit gets mentioned as a solid budget option. Community members appreciate the multiple locations and competitive pricing. The director is described as "customer-oriented" and willing to work with clients on custom solutions.

**The Reality Check:**

Like most hosting providers in this price range, Kuroit delivers what you pay for. The £3-5/month plans are perfect for small projects, development servers, or testing environments. If you're running mission-critical production systems, you might want to scale up to their higher-tier plans or consider managed services.

Their support response varies based on issue complexity and timing. Simple questions get answered quickly. Server-level issues during peak times? That might take longer.

The 72-hour refund policy gives you room to test things out without commitment, which is fair.
